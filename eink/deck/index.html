SEC.01

The Problem

value thrown away intact

value thrown away intact

We throw away material with most of its life left.

6.5%
Of the 5,152 kt of plastic Canada discards each year, only ~6.5% is reprocessed into new material; the rest is buried or burned.1
$0 in BC
No BC program buys post-industrial PS or ABS, the resins in every model kit. The nearest paying buyers sit roughly 4,000 km east, in Ontario.2
Oct 2027
BC's producer-funded vape take-back is legislated but unbuilt; no processor is named yet, even in Quebec's live program.4

The waste stream is not a disposal problem. It is mispriced inventory: sorted, concentrated, and delivered to a predictable set of doors every day.

SEC.02

the counter-attack

the counter-attack

riposte: the counter-attack after the parry.

Recover material at the point it is discarded, re-engineer it into durable products, and return it to the same community. Short loops, no ocean freight, value captured locally.

SEC.03

The Model

collect at the point of sale

collect at the point of sale

Supply we don't have to go looking for.

We host drop-off bins at the businesses whose products become the waste; the material returns through its own front door, pre-sorted and clean. Post-industrial resin like this is cleaner and more homogeneous than curbside plastic, so it commands a premium3; ours arrives free, because no BC buyer competes for PS or ABS today.2

PARTNER NETWORKRL-200
  • Hobby & model stores: gunpla runners; single-resin PS/ABS
  • Phone / IT repair: cells pulled during service
  • Vape & e-cig retailers: spent disposables
  • Electronics retail: take-back volume (e.g. Best Buy)
  • Supply is clean, sorted, and free at the door
SEC.04

Product Lines

two streams, one loop

two streams, one loop

Two feedstocks. Two product lines.

Plastic Works
Model-kit PS/ABS runners shredded, sorted, and injection-molded into durable goods; these are the resins BC has no buyer for,2 remade locally and sold locally.
Project HEX
Reclaimed lithium cells tested, graded, and rebuilt into modular hexagonal power tiles: the flagship product.

Plastic Works funds near-term revenue and the collection network; Project HEX is the venture-scale opportunity.

SEC.06

The Vision

a self-charging skin

a self-charging skin

Hexagons tessellate. So we clad the whole home.

Every tile generates, stores, and connects. Snap enough together and the array becomes the building's outer surface: a geodesic home that powers itself. The array scales without gaps, wraps compound curves, and grows one reclaimed tile at a time. The waste stream becomes distributed energy infrastructure.
