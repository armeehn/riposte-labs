Operations // DOC NO. RL-200-A

Our Process

We do not ask people to find us. We meet material where it already ends up, at the businesses it came from. Our collection runs on a network of local drop-off partners, each hosting a bin for the specific waste stream their customers generate.

SEC.01

The Drop-off Model

collect at the point of sale

We partner with local businesses as drop-off points for the recyclable materials that were originally part of a purchase they made.

SPEC / COLLECTION MODELRL-200
  • Model: hosted drop-off bins at partner businesses
  • Origin: material returns to its own point of sale
  • Radius: local. Collected here, processed here, used here
  • Sorting: streams kept separate at intake
  • Partner cost: none; we supply and service the bins

The material a business sells often comes back through its own door as waste: an offcut, a spent cell, an end-of-life device. Rather than build a separate collection system, we place a bin at that natural return point and close the loop where it starts.

For the customer, dropping off is as convenient as the original purchase. For the partner, it is a visible commitment to keeping material out of landfill. For us, it means a clean, predictable, pre-sorted supply.

SEC.02

Plastic Stream

runners & sprues from hobby stores

Our plastic feedstock comes from scale-model kits: the runners and sprues left over once the parts are clipped out.

SPEC / PLASTIC INTAKERL-P02
  • Source: hobby, model-kit, and game stores
  • Material: runners & sprues · polystyrene / ABS
  • Condition: clean, dry, uncontaminated by design
  • Sorting: separated by colour where possible
  • Market: PS/ABS · no BC buyer pays; nearest are in Ontario2
  • Destination: shred → sort → dry → injection mold

Every model kit (Gunpla and its relatives) arrives as parts held on a plastic lattice. Once a build is finished, that lattice is discarded. It is one of the cleanest waste streams available: uniform, high-grade polystyrene and ABS, moulded to a known spec, sorted by frame, and free of any food or liquid contamination.

Hobby and model stores are the ideal partner. Their customers generate a steady supply of this exact offcut, and many builders already save their runners rather than bin them. We place a collection bin in the shop; the material flows straight into our injection-molding loop.

Post-industrial resin like this is cleaner and more homogeneous than curbside plastic, so it commands a premium1, yet no BC program takes plastics from business, and no local buyer pays for PS or ABS2. That gap is our feedstock.

SEC.03

Battery Stream

cells from repair, vape & retail

Lithium cells accumulate wherever devices are sold or serviced. We partner with those businesses to intercept them.

SPEC / CELL INTAKERL-H02
  • Sources: phone / IT repair shops
  • Sources: e-cigarette & vape retailers
  • Sources: electronics retailers (e.g. Best Buy)
  • Material: lithium cells from phones, disposables, devices
  • Handling: terminals taped, stored in fire-safe containers
  • Transport: lithium dangerous goods · UN3480 / UN3481, Class 93
  • Destination: Project HEX reclamation + rebuild

End-of-life cells are hazardous in a curbside bin but valuable in the right hands. We collect them through the businesses that handle them every day and route them into Project HEX for testing, grading, and rebuilding into modular power units.

Three partner types supply this stream:

Phone & IT repair shops: cells pulled during battery replacements and board repairs, otherwise stockpiled or discarded.
E-cigarette & vape retailers: spent disposables and modules, each holding a lithium cell usually discarded with life still in it.
Electronics retailers: large stores such as Best Buy that already run take-back programs and gather cells at volume.

BC has no vape take-back today; a producer-funded program is legislated for October 2027, with plans due December 2026; no processor is named yet, even in Quebec's live program4. Discarded cells also drove 448 waste-facility fires across the US and Canada in 20255. Intercepting them now is both a safety measure and a head start.

All cells stored terminals-taped, fire-safe
