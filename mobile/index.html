Canada discards 5,152 kt of plastic a year and reprocesses only about 6.5%1 of it; batteries and devices are binned with most of their service life still inside. Riposte Laboratories was founded to address that directly: recovering material from the waste stream and returning it as durable, well-engineered products.

We operate at a local scale: recovering materials that would otherwise be discarded, engineering them into functional products, and returning them to the communities they came from.

SEC.02

Plastic Works

local loop injection molding

local loop injection molding

Plastic is collected from local sources, then shredded, sorted, and remanufactured through injection molding.

SPEC / PLASTIC LOOPRL-P01
  • Feedstock: clean post-industrial PS & ABS (model-kit runners)
  • Process: shred → sort → dry → injection mold
  • Output: durable repurposed goods, molded in-house
  • Radius: local. Collected here, remade here, used here
  • Principle: keep material in use, close to home

Post-industrial PS and ABS (the resins in every model kit) are clean, single-resin, and homogeneous2, yet no BC program takes plastics from business and no local buyer pays for them3. We keep that loop local: community-scale collection, in-house processing, and molded products returned to the area they came from.

Short supply loops. Long-lived products. No ocean freight.

SEC.03

Project HEX

modular reclaimed power

modular reclaimed power

Project HEX turns reclaimed lithium cells into a modular hexagonal power tile: an energy panel that clips into arrays and clads the surface of a building.

SPEC / HEX UNITRL-H01
  • Cells: 6 × cylindrical Li-ion, 45 × 13 mm · reclaimed and graded
  • Topology: one cell per edge, wired 6S (series)
  • Energy: 6 × 2.41 Wh = 14.46 Wh per tile
  • Top face: integrated photovoltaic panel · self-charging
  • Edges: magnetic · tiles snap into tessellating arrays
  • Accessory: magnet-mounted screen for interface + telemetry
  • Geometry: hexagonal, tessellates over geodesic structures
  • Status: in development · patent pending

Each unit carries six cylindrical cells measuring 45 × 13 mm, one seated along every edge of the hexagon and wired in series. Six reclaimed 2.41 Wh cells deliver 14.46 Wh per tile at a 6S output.

The top face is a solar panel, so every tile is a small generator that tops up its own cells. Magnetic edges let units snap together, and those same magnets accept an optional screen module to interface with the array.

Because hexagons tessellate, arrays scale without gaps and wrap compound curves; the geometry is built to sheath a geodesic-based home in self-charging power.

Patent pending · RL-H series
